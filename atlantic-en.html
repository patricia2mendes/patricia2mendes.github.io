ARTLANTIC DISTRIBUTION LDA

COO and Co-founder

Having the privilege to closely accompany a company from its foundation strengthened my professional experience in various aspects. Additionally, I highlight that the team was multi-generational and multinational, aspects that I greatly value due to the diversity of inputs.

Local creation, global heart: a startup that helps Madeiran artisans to market their artwork globally. It involves a rigorous research process, working with people from different backgrounds, eliciting a very strong social bond. This goal, in my view, is a key to the success of any company today: concern for people.

In the company, we always aimed to create a self-sustaining 'social ecosystem', where artisans focus on their dreams: they produce their works. Thus, in a preliminary phase, we dealt with consulting artisans (process optimization, innovative ideas in line with the market, among others) and analysis of the quality of the products themselves. In the business model, ARTLANTIC would order, buy, or resell its products to B2B customers (for example, hotels, wine companies, among others), or B2C customers on the marketplace (on Etsy and others).

In addition to this commercial aspect, of course, the products had to be well represented. For this purpose, we hired a South African photographer, and I prepared the design of some booklets to add to the final packaging and allow the products to 'tell' their story.
